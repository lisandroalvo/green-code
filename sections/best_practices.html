Best practices

As Developers or Designers, we should put in practice the best and most robust solutions

Here some good practices

Creativity

When it is time to create something totally new all by yourself. This can get quite intimidating for some first-timers, for others, it could be all excitement and eagerness to finally begin the real stuff… until they begin the real stuff.

Best Tools and concepts

UX tools do a lot of heavy lifting when it comes to user research and design. These tools can help you visualize, organize, and communicate your points to save time and money. There are many parameters to consider while selecting one tool. The ones you choose depend on the stage in the creation process, the size of your organization, and the type of digital product you’re designing or managing.

Developing good Habits

This is the number one habit that all great people seem to have, whether they’re chefs, designers, artists, speakers, philosophers, musicians, or really any profession. When interacting with something similar to what you do, break it down. How does it work? How could you recreate it? How could you make it work better? What do you love about it? What do you hate? By consciously breaking down the things that you see, even if they aren’t directly related to UX, you’re training your brain to be constantly active and taking everything apart, which means later, when you come across a similar problem, you’ll be able to synthesize a solution from some of the solutions you’ve seen elsewhere.

Reduce your footprint

It’s incredibly difficult to establish the full energy chain of internet usage because everyone has different devices, are operating in different temperatures and are all in different locations. Each website has multiple assets which may be hosted on a variety of different platforms. Each of these impact the energy consumption of your website.
